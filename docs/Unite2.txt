MANIA： meaning 'madness', a mental illness
- kleptomaniac: a mental illness in which a person has a strong desire to steal things.
'klepto' means 'to steal'
- dipsomaniac: a person who has strong desire to drink.'dipso-' means 'thirst'
- megalomaniac: a mental disorder marked by feelings of great personal power and importance
- egomaniac: someone who is extremely self-centered and ignores the problems and concerns of others

PSYCH:  meaning 'breath, soul, life'
- psyche: soul, personality, mind.
nation's psyche
- psychedelic: of or relating to drug that produces abnormal and often extreme mental effects
- psychosomatic: caused by mental or emotional problems rather than by physical illness
she has psychosomatic problems
- psychotherapist: / psychiatrist

CEPT: meaning to take, to seize
- reception: the act of receiving; a social gathering where guests are formally welcomed
- intercept: to stop, or interrupt
- perceptible: noticeable or able to be felt by the senses
- susceptible: open to some influence; able to be submitted to an action or process

FIN: meaning end or boundary
- confine: to keep someone or something within limits; to hold someone in a location
- definitive: specifying perfectly or precisely
- finite: having definite limits  | | infinite
- infinitesimal: extremely small

JECT：meaning "throw" or "hurl"
- interject: to interrupt a conversation
- conjecture: to guess, together to throw
- projection: an estimate of what might happen
- trajectory: "trans" meaning across, so we can say the trajectory of bullet

TRACT: meaning "drag" or "draw"
- traction: the friction
- retract: to pull back
- protracted: continued or extended
- intractable: not easily handled, led, taught

DUC/DUCT: to lead
- conducive: tending to promote, encourage, or assist 
- deduction: subtraction
- induce: bring about
- seduction: attraction or charm

SEQU: to follow
- sequential: arranged in order or in a series
- subsequent: following in time, subsequent days/years，subsequently(finally)
- consequential: resulting, important
- non sequitur: it does not follow
